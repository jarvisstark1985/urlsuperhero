How Branded Short Links Increase Click-Through Rates by 34%

Generic short links are leaving clicks on the table. Here is the data behind why branded URLs consistently outperform and how to make the switch today.

Published April 20, 2026 · 11 min read · By URL SuperHero Team

You have spent hours crafting the perfect email. The subject line is compelling. The copy is sharp. The offer is irresistible. Then you paste a link that looks like this: bit.ly/3xK9mPq. In that instant, you have undermined everything. Your audience sees a random string of characters from a domain that is not yours, and a significant percentage of them will not click.

The data is clear: branded short links outperform generic shorteners by an average of 34% in click-through rate. This is not a minor optimization. For a business sending millions of links per year, that difference translates to thousands of additional clicks, hundreds of additional conversions, and measurable revenue growth.

The 34% figure comes from aggregated performance data across marketing campaigns spanning email, social media, SMS, and paid advertising. The pattern is remarkably consistent across industries and channel types.

Why Branded Links Outperform: The Psychology

Trust and Recognition

When a user sees a link from your branded domain, they immediately recognize who is behind it. This recognition triggers familiarity and trust. A link from "go.yourcompany.com/spring-sale" tells the user exactly who is sending them somewhere and gives a hint about where they are going. A link from "bit.ly/3xK9mPq" tells them nothing. In an era of phishing attacks and online scams, that trust gap matters enormously.

Destination Clarity

Branded short links can include descriptive slugs that preview the destination content. Compare these two links:

Generic: bit.ly/3xK9mPq Branded: go.yourcompany.com/spring-sale

The branded version communicates three things: who is behind the link, that it is related to a spring sale, and that it is a legitimate business destination. The generic version communicates nothing. Users make click decisions in milliseconds, and that preview information makes the difference.

Brand Reinforcement

Every branded link is a micro-impression of your brand. When someone sees your domain in an email, on social media, in a text message, and in a partnership promotion, that repetition builds awareness and authority. Generic shorteners give that brand equity to the shortener platform instead of your business.

Spam Filter Performance

Email spam filters and social media platforms flag generic shortener domains because spammers abuse them. Branded domains have their own reputation, and if you maintain good sending practices, your links are less likely to be flagged or suppressed. This means more of your messages reach the inbox and more of your social posts get full visibility.

How to Implement Branded Short Links

Step 1: Choose Your Branded Domain

Your branded short domain should be short, memorable, and clearly connected to your brand. Common patterns include:

  • Subdomain approach: go.yourcompany.com, link.yourcompany.com
  • Shortened domain: yourco.link, brand.io
  • Country code domains: yourbrand.co, yourbrand.to

The best option for most businesses is a subdomain of your primary domain. It clearly associates the short link with your brand and inherits the trust of your main domain.

Step 2: Set Up DNS and SSL

Once you have your domain, configure DNS to point to your link management platform and provision an SSL certificate. Your links must use HTTPS. Any link management platform worth using handles SSL provisioning automatically.

Important: Never serve links over HTTP. Browsers flag non-HTTPS links as insecure, which destroys the trust advantage that branded links provide. Your link management platform should enforce HTTPS automatically.

Step 3: Create Your Link Naming Convention

The slug (the part after the domain) should be descriptive and consistent. Use human-readable slugs for public-facing links and auto-generated codes for high-volume tracking links:

Public-facing: go.yourcompany.com/spring-sale go.yourcompany.com/free-trial go.yourcompany.com/webinar High-volume: go.yourcompany.com/s/4kF9 go.yourcompany.com/s/7mQ2

Public-facing links benefit from descriptive slugs because users see them. High-volume links generated for individual email recipients or ad variations can use short codes because performance tracking is more important than readability.

Step 4: Migrate Existing Links

You do not need to switch everything at once. Start with your highest-visibility channels: email campaigns, social media bios, and SMS marketing. Measure the performance difference. Then roll out to lower-visibility channels like partner links, documentation, and internal communications.

Step 5: Monitor and Optimize

Track your branded link performance against your previous generic link metrics. The improvement should be visible within the first week. Pay attention to click-through rates by channel, geographic click patterns, device breakdown, and peak engagement times. Use these insights to optimize your campaigns beyond just the link itself.

Branded Links Across Marketing Channels

Email Marketing

Email is where branded links deliver the biggest impact. Every link in your email should use your branded domain. This applies to your primary CTA, secondary links, social icons, and footer links. Consistency builds trust. When recipients see your branded domain on every link, they develop confidence that your emails are safe to engage with.

Social Media

Social media platforms display link previews differently, but the URL is always visible. A branded link stands out in a feed full of generic shortened URLs. It signals professionalism and legitimacy. For platforms with character limits, branded short links also maximize the space available for your message.

SMS and Messaging

In SMS, trust is everything. Recipients are increasingly wary of clicking links in text messages because of smishing (SMS phishing) attacks. A branded link from a domain they recognize dramatically increases the likelihood of engagement. The 53% CTR improvement in SMS campaigns reflects this trust dynamic.

Print and Physical Media

Branded short links work beautifully on print materials, packaging, business cards, and signage. A clean, memorable URL like "go.yourcompany.com/menu" is easy to type manually, which matters when someone cannot click. Generic shortener links with random characters are nearly impossible to type accurately from a printed source.
